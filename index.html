Welcome!

Thank you for taking the time to visit this site (that I built from scratch) to learn about me and why I would be a great addition to your team at Flatiron School!

During the summers of 2014 and 2015, I served as the NYC Program Intern and Operations Intern, respectively, at Girls Who Code. Girls Who Code is an amazing non-profit organization with a mission very similar to that of Flatiron School: "To teach passionate, creative people (in GWC's case, middle and high school girls) how to code." Those two summers impacted me greatly and have lead me to where I am today, ready to continue what I started as a champion of tech education at Flatiron School.


I admire Flatiron School's commitment to helping everyone from recent high school graduates to non-graduates to career-changers achieve their professional and personal goals as programmers by granting scholarships, providing various course offerings that allow students to set their own pace, and by supporting them in their job search upon graduation.


Equipping people from all walks of life with the power of programming skills is an important and necessary undertaking that I am excited to promote. Seeing as Flatiron School is at the head of the pack in terms of disrupting the tech education landscape and diversifying the pool of talented coders around the world, I would cherish the opportunity to apply my passion and skills to execute and expand the company's mission.
